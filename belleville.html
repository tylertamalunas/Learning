History

Belleville first came to be by the donation of 1 acre of land by George Blair in 1814 for a Public Square. Afterward, the county seat was moved to Belleville to be more centrally located. The city became known for producing nails, printing presses, iron castins, stoves, and agricultural equipment after the Civil War. After the discovery of massive amounts of coal deposists nearby, many farmers became coal miners.

About

The city became known for producing nails, printing presses, iron castins, stoves, and agricultural equipment after the Civil War. It was nicknamed "The Stove Capital of the World" and is home to the first brewery in Illinois. The city is considered part of Greater St. Louis area. It is the eighth most populated city in Illinois.

Climate

Belleville is considered to have a humid subtropical climate. The summers in Bellevile are hot and humid. Winters are very cold and snowy. It is partly cloudy year round.

History

The area was first inhabited by the Potawatomi Native American tribe. It was first organized as a town of about 200 people, and grew quickly enough to be the fastest growing city for several decades. In 1871 a fire destroyed an area of the city about 4 miles long and 1 mile wide, it was later know as the Great Chicago Fire. The subsequent rebuilding of the city resulted in the worlds first skyscraper. Chicago became the nations hub for railroads, and developed the standardized system of North American time zones that are now used throughout the continent.

About

Chicago is the most populous city in Illinois, and third in the United States. It is bordered by one of the Great Lakes, Michigan. It is also the home to the first ever steel skyscraper. It is cnosidered an international hub for finance,commerce, education, and technology. It is also considered the railroad hub of the USA.

Climate

Chicago is considered to have a humid continental climate, and experiences all 4 seasons. Summers are hot and humid with frequent heat waves. Winters are cold and snowy, with blizzards. Spring and Autumn are mild and short with low humidity.

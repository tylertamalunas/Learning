History

The city was first settled in the late 1810s. It was first named "Calhoun", after a Senator from South Carolina. It was first settled by trappers and fur traders coming to the Sangamon River. The city renamed itself in 1832 to Springfield. Although not originally the capital of Illinois, it became so in 1832, and was the third city to be named the capital of Illinois.

About

Capital of Illinois. Seventh most populous city in Illinois. Largest city in central Illinois. The most historic resident from Springfield was President Abraham Lincoln.

Climate

Springfield is considered either a hot-summer humid continental climate, or a humid subtropical climate. The city is located at the end of the so-called "Tornado Alley", which results in frequent thunderstorms and possible tornadoes. The city averages 17.1 inches of snow every year.
